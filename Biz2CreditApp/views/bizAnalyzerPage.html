The Debt to Income ratio is calculated by adding your personal and business debt, and dividing it by your total income. Based on a review of your debt- to- income ratio, it seems that your personal debt payment as a proportion of your personal income is high. To keep your debt to credit ratio low, pay your credit card bills on time. Ensure that your Accounts Recievable and Account Payable are balanced. A longer time in business increases your chances of getting a loan as banks see that you have been operating for a long time and have overcome the challenging first few years of setting up a business. Lenders look at the kind of operating margins you have over time and understand the seasonality and cyclical nature of the business. If a business lasts the first 1000 days it has increased chances of surviving. Banks usually lend unsecured working capital lines and term loans to businesses which are over 2 years old and have well documented accounts receivables. In case of term loans, banks look for inventory, equipment, real estate, goodwill and any other hard assets to put a line against.

The longer you are in business, the better chances of you getting funding. Accounts receivables should be managed so that they should not exceed 90 days as that would make them ineligible for lines of credit against accounts receivables. Also the compensation of officers on the business tax returns should tally with the income on the personal tax returns.
